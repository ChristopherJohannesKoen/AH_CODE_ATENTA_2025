Assessment: Allergic food gastroenteritis and mild contact dermatitis due to food.

Plan:

Prescribe Cetirizine 10 mg once daily as needed to manage the rash.
Recommend oral rehydration solutions to prevent dehydration from diarrhea.
Monitor symptoms for resolution over the next few days.
Educate on signs to seek urgent care: swelling of lips/tongue, trouble breathing/swallowing, rapidly worsening rash, inability to keep fluids down, feeling very weak or faint.
Follow-up if symptoms persist or worsen after 3 days.

Medical Decision Making: Given the acute onset of symptoms shortly after ingesting the meal, along with an elevated white blood cell count of about 15,000 indicating a possible allergic response, an allergic reaction is highly probable. Tim has been advised to monitor his symptoms and return should they persist or escalate, ensuring the avoidance of suspected allergens in future meals.
